# Tactile Vault — 60-second Judge Brief

Tactile Vault is a compact five-finger MuJoCo system for retrieving emergency medicine from a tactile combination vault when smoke or darkness makes vision unreliable.

## Inspect first

1. `artifacts/demo.mp4` — generated by the submitted controller.
2. `artifacts/report.json` — final delivery result and controller counts.
3. `artifacts/evaluation.json` — 32 fixed-seed baseline-versus-residual rollouts.
4. `artifacts/policy_card.json` — observations, actions, feedback, and honest scope.
5. `scene.xml` — five fingers, 19 actuator channels, free body, collision geometry, touch/frame/joint sensors.

## Why it is easy to score

- The task has seven visible stages and quantitative completion criteria.
- The dial follows a three-direction tactile code; the hand then confirms the latch, forms an opposed five-finger grasp, rejects an injected slip, and delivers the vial.
- Every frame is driven from one deterministic seed and every output is regenerated by one command.
- Controller state is exposed in the video and JSON rather than asserted only in prose.
- The high-level stage prior and post-contact vial attachment are explicitly documented; sensor residuals, actuators, joints, collisions, and stress evaluation are directly inspectable.
